Why Co-Extrusion

A polymer cap, fused to the core.

Uncapped WPC absorbs water and tends to fade within a few summers. Co-extruded WPC is made differently: a sealed PE polymer skin is bonded around the entire core during manufacture, so UV, water and salt air don't reach the wood fibre.

  1. 01

    UV-stable surface

    Pigment is locked into the polymer skin during co-extrusion, so there is no surface coating to chip or chalk. Tested for 5,000+ hours under accelerated AU sun.

  2. 02

    360° PE co-ex cap

    The sealed polyethylene skin wraps every face (top, bottom and both sides), so water, salt air and dirt never reach the wood fibre. Standard WPC only caps three sides.

  3. 03

    Molecular bond

    Cap and core are extruded simultaneously and fused at 195°C. There is no glue line to peel; the two layers are chemically welded into one board.

  4. 04

    Dense WPC core

    Recycled hardwood fibres compressed with HDPE polymer. Twice the density of standard WPC, which is why Fencly boards span 1.83m without sag.
